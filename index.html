What is CGPA?

The Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is a standard measure used to evaluate a student’s overall academic performance over a period, usually across semesters. It is calculated as the weighted average of grade points earned in all subjects, factoring in the credit hours of each course. 

Formula:

CGPA = (Σ (Grade Points × Credit Hours)) / (Σ Credit Hours)

How to Convert CGPA to Percentage: The Most Trusted Method

Converting CGPA to percentage helps students and recruiters interpret academic scores more clearly. While conversion formulas vary across universities, the most widely accepted formula in Indian universities is: 

Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

Example:

If your CGPA is 8.4, your percentage is: 

Percentage = 8.4 × 9.5 = 79.8%

*Note: Always check your university’s official conversion formula to ensure accuracy.*

Convert Percentage to CGPA Effortlessly

If you have your percentage score and want to find the equivalent CGPA, use this simple reverse formula: 

CGPA = Percentage / 9.5

Example:

For a percentage of 85%, your CGPA will be: 

CGPA = 85 / 9.5 ≈ 8.95

What are GPA and SGPA? Explained with Examples

Grade Point Average (GPA) measures your academic performance in a specific semester or term. It averages the grade points earned across all subjects during that period.

Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) is similar to GPA but might incorporate specific institutional regulations. Both GPA and SGPA are essential for understanding semester-wise performance.

Our CGPA calculator helps you compute your cumulative score based on multiple SGPA or GPA values weighted by credit hours.

GPA and SGPA Calculations

  • GPA (Grade Point Average): Average grade points in a single term or semester.
  • SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average): Semester-specific GPA which may have institution-specific calculation nuances.

To calculate overall CGPA from multiple semesters, you can average the SGPAs: 

CGPA = (Sum of all SGPAs) / Number of Semesters

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is the difference between CGPA and GPA?
CGPA is the cumulative average of all semesters, while GPA or SGPA is calculated for individual semesters.
2. Is CGPA the same across all universities?
No, grading scales and CGPA calculations can vary. Always refer to your university’s official guidelines.

Yes, converting CGPA to percentage helps recruiters understand your academic performance better.
